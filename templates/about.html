Author's Background

After studying marketing Melissa Yasmina Chauffour worked as a search engine optimization analyst and discovered her interest in coding.

She chose the University of Birmingham to do her MSc in Computer Science. It is in the context of her studies that she is creating this web application.

Passioned about crime since a young age, Melissa chose to focus on the crime rate in the West Midlands as her MSc dissertation subject.

Data Provenance

The data comes from data.police.uk which is a site for open data about crime and policing in England, Wales and Northern Ireland. We concentrated our efforts on the West Midlands region and its four police forces:

  • Staffordshire Police
  • Warwickshire Police
  • West Mercia Police
  • West Midlands Police

    • Two datasets were used, street-level crime and stop and search between January 2011 and March 2021.

    The following table gives a detailed description of the crime types in the dataset

    Crime Type Description
    Anti-social behaviour Includes personal, environmental and nuisance anti-social behaviour.
    Bicycle theft Includes the taking without consent or theft of a pedal cycle.
    Burglary Includes offences where a person enters a house or other building with the intention of stealing.
    Criminal damage and arson Includes damage to buildings and vehicles and deliberate damage by fire.
    Drugs Includes offences related to possession, supply and production.
    Other crime Includes forgery, perjury and other miscellaneous crime.
    Other theft Includes theft by an employee, blackmail and making off without payment.
    Possession of weapons Includes possession of a weapon, such as a firearm or knife.
    Public order Includes offences which cause fear, alarm or distress.
    Robbery Includes offences where a person uses force or threat of force to steal.
    Shoplifting Includes theft from shops or stalls.
    Theft from the person Includes crimes that involve theft directly from the victim (including handbag, wallet, cash, mobile phones) but without the use or threat of physical force.
    Vehicle crime Includes theft from or of a vehicle or interference with a vehicle.
    Violence and sexual offences Includes offences against the person such as common assaults, Grievous Bodily Harm, and sexual offences.
    All crime Total for all categories.

    The following table gives a detailed description of each feature used

    Feature Description
    Month The date the crime was committed (e.g. 2021-03).
    Longitude The anonymised coordinates of the crime.
    Latitude The anonymised coordinates of the crime.
    Location Where the crime occurred (specific or near location).
    LSOA name The name of the Lower Layer Super Output Area where the crime occurred.
    Crime type One of the crime types.
    Legislation The legislation linked to the object of search.
    Object of search The object searched for during the stop and search.
    Outcome The outcome of the stop and search.

    The West Midlands Region

    The West Midlands Region - Overview

    The West Midlands is one of the nine official regions of England with a 2014 estimated population of 5,713,000 which makes it the second most populous region in England.

    The region consists of the following counties:
    Herefordshire, Shropshire, Staffordshire, Warwickshire, Worcestershire and the West Midlands.

    The region is known for its high rate of crime which is the second highest after London. In fact, between May 2020 and April 2021, the West Midlands has ranked 1st in possession of weapons crime, 2nd in vehicle crime and 3rd in burglary and violent crime.
